## Variant A - Team-woven

Per-slot integrated-circuit (IC) machine drive sits at the junction of three disciplines — monolithic power-IC design, aerospace electrical-machine design and power-electronics thermal management — and cannot be delivered if any one is missing. This team holds all three at published, experimentally validated level. Dr Mehdi Baghdadi (Principal Investigator (PI), UCL Advanced Propulsion Laboratory) leads the silicon and power-conversion strand and work packages (WPs) 2 and 3; Dr Pedram Asef (Co-Investigator (Co-I)) leads electromagnetic machine design in WP1; Dr Marilize Everts (Co-I) leads thermal co-design in WP2 and WP4. The evidence below is organised around what each work package demands.

### Contributions to the generation of new ideas, tools, methodologies or knowledge

WP2 rests on a claim few teams can make: a complete per-slot drive already exists in silicon. The PI's group conceived, fabricated and measured the binary-tree multilevel converter (BTMLC) IC, which integrates the entire per-slot drive infrastructure — power switches, gate drivers, floating supplies, level shifters and dead-time generation — on a single 15.2 mm² die in 130 nm bipolar-CMOS-DMOS (BCD) technology, delivering 2 A at up to ~26 V from DC to 5 kHz (IEEE Transactions on Power Electronics, accepted 2026). Companion silicon in the same process demonstrated the multiple-output level shifting with input-side dead-time control that floating-domain per-slot drive requires, achieving a 17-times reduction in propagation delay and a 22-to-66-times reduction in dead-time spread (IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems I, accepted 2026). Both chips descend from the PI's DCAT adiabatic DC-AC converter (PCIM Europe, 2016), a Google/IEEE Little Box Challenge finalist that established the programme's founding principle — magnetic distribution of a high bus voltage into low-voltage sections — at measured efficiency above 99% and power density of approximately 400 W/in³. Doctoral research in the group has since driven industrial induction machines with converters of this binary-tree family under closed-loop control, demonstrating that the architecture operates into machine loads with back-electromotive force: the direct experimental precedent for the question WP2 answers at per-slot scale.

WP1 demands machine-design capability of matching depth. Co-I Asef originated a multitooth interactive flux-reversal permanent-magnet motor and carried it from genetic-algorithm optimisation through prototyping to experimental validation within approximately 4% of finite-element analysis (FEA) predictions (IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics, accepted 2026 [PI TO CONFIRM: volume/issue/pages/DOI]) — the same origination-to-validation cycle WP1 executes for the per-slot open-end winding. His comparative study of fault-tolerant dual three-phase permanent-magnet synchronous machines (PMSMs) with modular windings for electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) aircraft — a 48-slot/8-pole machine analysed under healthy, open-circuit and short-circuit conditions through a multi-institutional collaboration [PI TO CONFIRM: venue and partner institutions] — established the winding-factor and fault-analysis methodology WP1 extends to slot-level granularity, in exactly the machine class and application this programme targets.

WP2 and WP4 require thermal co-design at power-electronics heat fluxes, because per-slot IC modules must dissipate inside a winding environment at 120–150 °C. Co-I Everts' microchannel heat-sink research characterised hybrid enhanced architectures managing heat fluxes of 100 W/cm² [PI TO CONFIRM: venue/year/DOI] — the analysis methodology the programme applies to per-slot module packaging. WP1 and WP3 additionally draw on the group's validated analytical models of multi-winding converter dynamics and its experimentally demonstrated, sensorless balancing methods, both published in IEEE Transactions on Power Electronics in 2025.

### The development of others and maintenance of effective working relationships

This programme trains two postdoctoral research associates (PDRAs) across silicon, machine and thermal disciplines, and the supervision model it uses is already proven. The PI has supervised approximately 12 doctoral researchers as first supervisor, six of whom have completed, and has co-supervised approximately six more, spanning power electronics, electrical machines, integrated converters and electric propulsion. Development shows in outputs and destinations: doctoral researchers in the group publish in IEEE Transactions on Power Electronics and IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems I during their programmes — including the BTMLC IC and the level-shifter silicon on which this proposal builds — and progress to postdoctoral and faculty-adjacent positions in the UK and Europe. EPSRC studentship EP/T517793/1 funded the doctoral research that delivered the BTMLC platform, so this proposal carries a prior EPSRC investment forward through the researcher pipeline that investment created. The PDRAs on this programme inherit that model: joint supervision across all three investigators, structured silicon-to-system training spanning WP1–WP4, and first-author publication targets in the same venues.

The team's working relationships extend beyond UCL and are maintained through delivery rather than correspondence. The eVTOL machine study evidences a sustained multi-institutional research collaboration in exactly the machine class the programme addresses [PI TO CONFIRM: partner institutions], and the SkyDrive programme — 100 kW, 18,000 RPM integrated aerospace electric propulsion developed with industrial partners ARC Additive and iNetic — evidences working industrial relationships at the power class this architecture ultimately serves.

### Contributions to the wider research and innovation community

A programme founding a new class of machine drive must sit inside the community that will scrutinise, reproduce and extend it. The PI and both Co-Is review for IEEE Transactions on Power Electronics, IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics, IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems and IET Power Electronics — the venues in which the programme's outputs will appear. [PI TO CONFIRM: editorial board memberships, conference committee roles, panel memberships and keynote lectures for all three investigators.] The group's publication practice supports reproduction rather than announcement: its silicon papers report complete measured data — die dimensions, path resistance, switching losses and thermal behaviour — at the level of detail other groups need to build on the platform, and this programme continues that practice with the open demonstrator dataset described in the next module.

### Contributions to broader research or innovation-users and audiences and towards wider societal benefit

Foundational research reaches society only through users, and the team's routes to users already function. The SkyDrive programme places the PI's group in a working relationship with the UK propulsion supply chain, through ARC Additive and iNetic, at the 100 kW, 18,000 RPM scale this architecture ultimately serves, and provides the industrial context into which the programme's technology readiness level (TRL) 1–3 outputs feed. Engagement with Eaton and Airbus [PI TO CONFIRM: letters of support] provides the translation route to TRL 4–6 development through Innovate UK or Aerospace Technology Institute mechanisms. WP4 commits the complete demonstrator dataset — efficiency maps, thermal characterisation, fault-insertion measurements and torque-ripple data — to open publication in the UCL institutional repository, so the first experimental record of a per-slot driven machine belongs to the whole community rather than to one firm. The societal case runs through the UK Jet Zero strategy: partial-discharge-free, gearbox-free, gracefully degrading electric propulsion is foundational to net-zero aviation by 2050, and this team combines, in one group, the silicon, machine and thermal capability that foundation requires.
