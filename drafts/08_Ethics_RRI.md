## EPSRC Standard Grant: "Foundations of Monolithically Integrated Per-Slot Machine Drives for Reconfigurable Aerospace Propulsion"

## VARIANT A — AREA framework

This programme raises no ethical issues requiring formal ethical approval: it involves no human participants, no animal research, and no collection or processing of personal data. Its responsible research and innovation (RRI) obligations are nonetheless real, and the team will manage them through the AREA framework — Anticipate, Reflect, Engage, Act.

**Anticipate.** The architecture targets civil electric propulsion in support of the UK Jet Zero strategy, and the team has considered how outputs could be used beyond that intent. Aerospace propulsion technology can be dual-use. Three features bound that risk: the programme operates at Technology Readiness Levels (TRL) 1–3, far below any deployable system; its outputs are pre-competitive design methodology and bench-scale evidence, not flight hardware; and every output will be published openly, so no party gains privileged access. **Reflect.** The strongest RRI contribution is the research aim itself: eliminating partial discharge by voltage architecture removes a barrier to lighter, more reliable electric propulsion and therefore to aviation decarbonisation. The team will also hold the research process to the same standard. The demonstrator is deliberately sized at 5–15 kW — the smallest scale at which the physics can be validated — minimising material use and test energy; prototype hardware will be reused across work packages and recycled at end of life under the Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment (WEEE) regulations.

**Engage.** Open science maximises the societal return on public investment. The complete demonstrator dataset, models and methods will be published openly, so any UK research group or manufacturer can build on the results without negotiation. Continuing engagement with the project partners [PI TO CONFIRM: Eaton, Airbus letters of support] and with the academic community through publication and presentation provides external scrutiny of the programme's direction and claims.

**Act.** Laboratory work involves 270 V direct-current (DC) bench systems and rotating machinery at up to 10,000 RPM. UCL risk assessment and the Advanced Propulsion Laboratory's operating procedures govern all experimental activity [PI TO CONFIRM: specific institutional safety, risk-assessment and approval processes]. Postdoctoral Research Associate (PDRA) recruitment will run through open competition with inclusive advertising and selection practice in line with UCL equality, diversity and inclusion policy. Research integrity follows UCL's research governance framework, with all data managed under the programme's data management plan. RRI is not a one-off statement: it stands as a fixed agenda item at each milestone review in the project management cycle, so anticipation and reflection are repeated as the evidence base develops rather than performed once at the outset.

## VARIANT B — Thematic structure

**Research integrity and data.** The team will conduct the programme under UCL's research governance framework, with all experimental data curated under a data management plan and the complete demonstrator dataset, models and methods published openly. Open publication maximises the societal return on public investment: any UK research group or manufacturer can build on the results without negotiation.
